Mango Cultivation

Mango cultivation is a labor-intensive agricultural practice that has been cherished for centuries across tropical and subtropical regions worldwide. The process begins with selecting suitable soil, preferably well-drained and fertile, and planting mango seedlings during the warm months. As the mango trees grow, they require regular watering and occasional pruning to maintain their shape and health. These sun-loving trees flourish in full sunlight, essential for fruit development. Farmers must protect the trees from pests and diseases through careful monitoring and employing eco-friendly pest control methods. Mangoes typically take several months to mature, with their vibrant hues and enticing aroma indicating ripeness. Harvesting involves hand-picking the ripe fruits delicately to avoid damage. With its rich flavor, juiciness, and nutritional benefits, mangoes have become a favorite fruit worldwide, making mango cultivation a vital source of income for many farming communities.
